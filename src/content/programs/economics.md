The Economics program offers both a Bachelor of Arts and a Bachelor of Science, each providing rigorous training in economic theory, quantitative methods, and data analysis.

## Program Highlights

- Both degrees are STEM-designated, reflecting the quantitative rigor of the program
- Small class sizes with dedicated faculty mentors
- Research opportunities with faculty on topics ranging from labor economics to international development
- Strong foundation for graduate study in economics, law, public policy, or business

## B.A. vs. B.S.

The **B.A. in Economics** offers more flexibility for electives and a second major, pairing well with political science, philosophy, or international studies. The **B.S. in Economics** emphasizes advanced mathematics and econometrics, ideal for students pursuing graduate school or quantitative careers.

## Career Outcomes

Economics graduates pursue careers in data analytics, financial services, public policy, consulting, government agencies, and research institutions.
