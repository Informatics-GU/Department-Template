The Accounting program at Gonzaga's School of Business Administration prepares students for successful careers in public accounting, corporate finance, government, and nonprofit organizations.

## Program Highlights

- AACSB-accredited in both business and accounting — a distinction held by fewer than 2% of business schools worldwide
- Hands-on experience through the Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) program
- Strong CPA exam pass rates
- Direct pathway to the Master in Accountancy (MAcc) program

## Career Outcomes

Graduates pursue careers as auditors, tax accountants, financial analysts, forensic accountants, and controllers at firms ranging from the Big Four to regional and local practices.

## Requirements

The B.B.A. in Accounting requires completion of the university core curriculum, the business core, and the accounting major courses. Students also complete a capstone course integrating business knowledge with ethical decision-making in the Jesuit tradition.
