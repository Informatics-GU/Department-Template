The Gonzaga University School of Business Administration has earned reaccreditation from AACSB International — Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business. The accreditation extends to both the school's business and accounting programs.

AACSB accreditation is the hallmark of excellence in business education and has been earned by fewer than 6% of the world's business schools. Dual accreditation in both business and accounting is held by fewer than 2% of schools worldwide.

## A Rigorous Process

Reaccreditation involves a comprehensive peer review process examining the school's mission, faculty qualifications, learning goals, and continuous improvement efforts.

"Earning reaccreditation confirms that our programs meet the highest global standards," said Dean Anderson. "It is a testament to the quality of our faculty and the rigor of our curriculum."

## What This Means for Students

For current and prospective students, AACSB accreditation means:

- Curriculum that meets globally recognized standards of quality
- Faculty who are active researchers and practitioners in their fields
- A degree that is recognized and respected by employers worldwide
- Access to a network of AACSB-accredited schools for exchange and graduate study opportunities
