The Jack H. Hemmingson Finance Lab in the Jepson Center has expanded its capabilities with the addition of four new Bloomberg terminals, bringing the total to eight.

The expansion gives more students hands-on access to the same professional-grade financial data and analytics tools used by investment banks, asset managers, and corporate finance teams around the world.

## Real-World Experience

Students in finance courses use the Bloomberg terminals to analyze markets, build financial models, and conduct equity research. The lab also supports students pursuing Bloomberg Market Concepts (BMC) certification, a credential recognized by employers in the financial industry.

"There's no substitute for working with real data on real platforms," said Dr. Maimuna Akter, Assistant Professor of Finance. "The Bloomberg terminals bridge the gap between classroom theory and professional practice."

## Open to All Business Students

While primarily used in finance courses, the lab is available to all students in the School of Business Administration for research and coursework.
