A team of three Gonzaga business students took first place at the Pacific Northwest Collegiate Business Plan Competition, earning a $10,000 prize for their startup concept.

The team, all members of the Hogan Entrepreneurial Leadership Program, pitched a technology platform designed to connect small businesses with sustainable supply chain partners.

## The Winning Pitch

The students developed their idea over the course of a semester, working with mentors from the Hogan Center for Entrepreneurship. Their pitch was praised by judges for its market research rigor, clear financial projections, and social impact potential.

"The Hogan program gave us the tools and the confidence to compete at this level," said one team member. "We learned how to think like entrepreneurs and communicate our vision effectively."

## About the Hogan Program

The Hogan Entrepreneurial Leadership Program is open to students across all majors at Gonzaga. It provides coursework, mentorship, and funding opportunities for students interested in creating new ventures in the private and public sectors.
